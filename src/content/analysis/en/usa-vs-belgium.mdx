## Opening: Both Sides Over 2.0 — The Market Has No Faith in This One

Just look at the odds and you'll know how little faith the market has in this fixture. A home win at 2.55 and an away win at 2.75 mean that whichever side you back, a win pays out more than double.

And the reason this match can carry such odds is, above all, down to Belgium. On paper, a traditional European powerhouse ranked ninth in the world facing a USA side that is a relative football underdog ranked 16th should be a comfortable win. But Belgium's dismal form through the group stage and knockouts has left everyone with a giant question mark over them.

## Belgium: They Beat Senegal 3-2, but the Process Was Scarier Than the Scoreline

Yes, Belgium did beat [Senegal](wiki:Senegal_national_football_team) 3-2, scoring three goals — the attack looks fine, right?

But watch the actual game: they didn't score for the first 85 minutes, and for a long stretch they were trailing 0-2. It was only in the dying moments — the 86th and 89th minutes — that they scrambled two goals to level, before winning it in extra time off a won penalty. Look at Belgium's squad — is that the performance they should be putting up?

They have [Kevin De Bruyne](wiki:Kevin_De_Bruyne) and [Romelu Lukaku](wiki:Romelu_Lukaku). Yes, they're old, and inconsistency has always been part of their story. But what about [Jérémy Doku](wiki:Jérémy_Doku)? He's only 24, one of the best wing dribblers on the planet. He produced some lovely runs, but none of them turned into goals — Belgium simply can't seem to unlock his full impact.

## USA: Full Strength, Electric at Home, and Plenty of Firepower

As for the USA, there were earlier fears that losing the red-carded [Folarin Balogun](wiki:Folarin_Balogun) would badly hurt their front line. But with his suspension deferred, the USA can now field a full-strength lineup.

The front pairing of [Christian Pulisic](wiki:Christian_Pulisic) and Balogun, alongside a midfield of [Weston McKennie](wiki:Weston_McKennie) and [Tyler Adams](wiki:Tyler_Adams), has delivered strong displays in every game bar the meaningless one against Turkey. With the boost of playing at home, they look genuinely formidable.

Consider this: [France](wiki:France_national_football_team)'s star-studded front line could only manage a single penalty against Paraguay, while the USA put four past that same Paraguay. Granted, the crudeness of Paraguay's defending varied wildly across those two games — but it still tells you something: the USA's firepower this tournament is absolutely for real.

## Key Battle: Belgium Are Unpredictable, but This Time All Three Views Line Up

On the prediction, honestly, Belgium are incredibly hard to read. They're full of miracles and instability — they can go 80 minutes without scoring, then bang in two goals in three minutes. They could only draw with weaker Egypt and Iran, yet they beat Senegal.

But this time my view lines up surprisingly well with both AIs: we all think the USA can leverage home advantage to beat Belgium inside 90 minutes, 2-1 or 2-0 — barring, of course, a miracle.

On balance, I'm with the hosts. The USA are at full strength, their firepower has clicked, and they have the home crowd behind them. Belgium always carry the chance of rolling a miracle, but as long as there's no miracle here, the USA should get the job done.
