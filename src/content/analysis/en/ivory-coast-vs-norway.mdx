## Pre-Match Context

We've already covered Norway in an earlier piece, and if you've been following this tournament at all, you're probably already very familiar with them — even if you haven't watched [Erling Haaland](wiki:Erling_Haaland) or [Martin Ødegaard](wiki:Martin_Ødegaard) play, you've almost certainly scrolled past a Haaland meme or two. The man — arguably the best striker in football right now, and inarguably its top meme generator — was red-hot through the group stage, helping Norway cruise through their first two matches and lock up qualification early. He then sat out the third match against France entirely — no reported injury concerns, just what looks like a straightforward rest decision.

That said, as we noted before, Norway's squad leans heavily attack-over-defense. In that France match, their back line let [Ousmane Dembélé](wiki:Ousmane_Dembélé) become the second-fastest player in World Cup history to score a hat-trick — which tells you exactly how shaky that defense really is.

## Ivory Coast: A Spine Full of 180cm-Plus Physical Specimens, With Real Tools to Trouble Haaland

[Ivory Coast](wiki:Ivory_Coast_national_football_team) are the reigning African Cup of Nations champions (2023 edition, though it was actually played in 2024). They might not be a household name to casual World Cup viewers, but their footballing quality is genuinely top-tier.

Up front they have [Sébastien Haller](wiki:Sébastien_Haller), a target man plying his trade in the Eredivisie — many World Cup-only fans probably know him better for beating testicular cancer than for football, but he was the standout player of that 2023 AFCON title run. On the wing there's Ligue 1 talent [Simon Adingra](wiki:Simon_Adingra). At the back, two Serie A defenders, [Evan Ndicka](wiki:Evan_Ndicka) and [Odilon Kossounou](wiki:Odilon_Kossounou), bring the kind of physicality that could give Haaland real problems. In fact, Ivory Coast's entire spine is built from physical, combative players standing over 180cm — giving them genuine tools to go toe-to-toe with him.

## Key Battleground: An Attacking Slugfest — Who Cracks First?

The odds lean toward Norway, but this one is genuinely close — both sides may struggle to contain the other's attack. The real question isn't "who can score," it's "whose defense breaks first." Ivory Coast's physically imposing spine makes them one of the toughest tests Haaland has faced this tournament; but Norway's own defense is just as fragile, and if Ivory Coast can take their chances, this match has every ingredient for a shootout.
