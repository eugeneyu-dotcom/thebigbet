## Opening: Baseball Is a Pitcher's Game

Dodgers vs Yankees, the classic interleague showdown. Baseball, at its core, is a pitcher's game, and the direction of this one depends largely on the starting-rotation matchup. On the moneyline the Dodgers are 1.75 and the Yankees 2.05 — the market clearly trusts the home side more.

## Dodgers: Lineup Depth and Home Advantage

The Dodgers' lineup depth is among the best in the league; even if the top of the order gets shut down, the middle and bottom can still manufacture a threat. Combined with playing at home and a more comfortable bullpen setup, that's the main reason they're favored on the moneyline.

## Yankees: The Power Is Still There, but Pitching Fatigue Is a Concern

The Yankees' power is unquestionable — one swing can change a game. But if their starter can't eat enough innings, an overworked bullpen on the road is vulnerable to the Dodgers' deep lineup late in the game.

## Betting Advice: Lean Dodgers

Weighing the starters, lineup depth and home advantage, I lean Dodgers. But a single baseball game carries huge variance — one walk-off hit can flip everything — so keep your stakes in check.
