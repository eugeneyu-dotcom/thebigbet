## Opening: A Clash of the World's Top Four, Decided by Pure Quality and the Moment

The World Cup has come down to the final four teams, and these four happen to be exactly the current FIFA world's top four ranked sides — so the quality gap is bound to be razor-thin. Lay out all four squads on paper and none of them have any glaring weakness. On top of that, all four are World Cup regulars whose players are either club teammates or have faced each other many times at club level, and the three European sides face off constantly at the Euros too — everyone knows everyone inside out. Winning through some surprise tactic isn't really on the table at this point. This far in, it comes down to pure quality, plus how you perform on the day and your recent form.

## France: Yet to Concede, Attack as the Best Defense

On form, [France](wiki:France_national_football_team) are unquestionably the best of the final four. Aside from the Paraguay game, where tough — some would say downright dirty — physical play denied them a goal from open play, every other match saw them score in the early-to-middle stages, and since entering the knockouts they still haven't conceded a single goal. Across the whole tournament they've shipped just two.

Plenty of people worried beforehand about the space behind their fullbacks and about [Mbappé](wiki:Kylian_Mbappé)'s defensive work, but France have proven that attack is the best defense — apply enough pressure with your attack, and force the opponent to keep tracking back all day, and they simply can't hurt you. What's more, France's front line is stacked with talent; even their substitutes bring plenty of attacking threat when they come on. So are you waiting for their legs to go? Forget it — their attacking resources could take you the full 120 minutes into extra time without a problem.

## Spain: Yamal's 8-2 Record Against Mbappé Is Mostly a Club Stat

As for [Spain](wiki:Spain_national_football_team), they did beat France at Euro 2024, and [Lamine Yamal](wiki:Lamine_Yamal)'s record against Mbappé over the past two years stands at a dominant 8 wins to 2 losses. But a large chunk of that is really a club-level scoreline. Real Madrid have come away with nothing over the past two seasons, with poor on-pitch cohesion and dressing-room turmoil off it that's hardly news anymore. Facing a united France side, can Spain still hold the edge and replay their 2024 win over France?

I'd say the key factor beyond Yamal is [Nico Williams](wiki:Nico_Williams)'s form. A big part of Spain's excellent results in recent years has come from the threat and tactics of having both wings firing at once. In the matches Nico Williams missed, it's clear that with just Yamal alone, Spain's attack drops off noticeably — he often faces 1-on-2 or even 1-on-3 out wide, unable to fully unleash his strongest weapon, the 1-v-1 dribble. That's why in two straight matches Spain could only rely on substitute [Mikel Merino](wiki:Mikel_Merino) to come up big, advancing on two somewhat fortunate goals.

## Key Battle: Nico Williams Is Back from Injury — How Much Can He Give?

Nico Williams already came off the bench last time, and he's expected to be available again this time — but fresh off injury, how much impact can he really provide? That's going to be the key to this match.

## Betting Advice: A Close Match Where the Odds Barely Matter — I Back France to Get Through in 90

In a match this evenly matched, the odds stop mattering much — a gap this small basically doesn't reflect the real difference between the two sides. As for my own call, with Nico Williams's condition in doubt, I think France have the slightly better chance, and I back them to get through within 90 minutes.
