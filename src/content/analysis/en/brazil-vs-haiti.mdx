## Pre-Match Context

After drawing their opener against Morocco, Brazil desperately need a win from their remaining two group stage matches. With points already squandered, three points here are essentially non-negotiable for the Seleção.

## Current Form & Team Analysis

Brazil have declined considerably since the Neymar era faded. They remain a top-tier side on paper, but expectations of a deep title run have been seriously tempered. Their current talisman, [Vinícius Júnior](wiki:Vinícius_Júnior), is arguably the most destructive left-winger in world football right now — yet his performances in a Brazil shirt have consistently fallen short of his club-level brilliance. Factor in a dismal World Cup qualifying campaign and a mid-cycle managerial change, and it is clear that Brazil are no longer the dominant force they once were.

As someone who watched the [7-1 Mineirão disaster](wiki:Mineirazo) unfold, I have always maintained a healthy skepticism about this Brazil side. Their back-to-back quarterfinal exits in the last two World Cups suggest that skepticism is well-founded.
